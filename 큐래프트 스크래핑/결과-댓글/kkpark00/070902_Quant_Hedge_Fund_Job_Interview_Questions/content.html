Few interview questions I encountered during my job searching days (about 3 years ago)

 

(1) Let's assume that we are drawing a pair of real random numbers from 2 independent uniform distributions. What is the probability that the sum of the two number will be greater than 1? How about a probability that the product of the two numbers will be less than 0.5? How about a probability that the average of the two numbers will be greater than 0.5?

 

(2) How would you price a call option at T0 with strike fixed at S(T1) and maturing in T2 where T0 < T1 < T2 and S(T1) is the underlying price at T1?

 

(3) When pricing options, what allows us to use risk free rate drift for the underlying asset price dynamics?

 

(4) Roll a die until you get a 6. What is the average number of times you will have to roll?

 

(5) Assume volatility is stochastic and negatively correlated to underlying asset returns, what implied vol shape do you expect?

 

(6) Under efficient markets, can you form a portfolio of options with positive gamma and negative vega?

 

(7) Under efficient markets, can you form a portfolio of options with positive theta and gamma?

 

(8) Assume X,Y,Z are standard normal random variables with some correlation structure as follows Corr(X,Y) = 0.8, Corr(Y,Z) = 0.8. What is the possible range of corr(X,Z)?
